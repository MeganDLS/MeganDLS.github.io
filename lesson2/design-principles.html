White Space &
Clean Design

Found on the National Geographic Webiste.

Published by editors of National Geographic.

The white space, in black, helps to keep the home page clean and sleek. Allowing separation between the different articles keeps the design simple and appealing. The proper use of white space on this site draws the reader in to learn more. The white space helps to highlight the alignment and brings the focus to the images on the page. Making the header white and the background of the main content black also sets this website apart from others.

nationalgeographic.com

Visual
Hierarchy

Found on the Overdrive Website. Announcing the Libby application for libraries.

Designed by Overdrive.

The visual hierarchy on this page draws the readers attention to the row of colorful book covers. The unique layout of the books and white background catches the reader's eye first. Second, the reader will go the largest font heading just beneath the books. After the heading, the reader will go on to view the information in the middle column, because it is unique to it's surroundings blocks of information. Finally, the reader will view the smaller images and corresponding information.

Overdrive.com

PARC:
Repetition

Found on the National Public Radio Webpage

Scott Montgomery is managing editor of NPR Digital News. Zach Brand is vice president of NPR Digital Media. Patrick Cooper is senior product manager of NPR.org

Repetition can be seen with the news articles and corresponding images. The images are the same size, and aligned appropriately. The titles are all the same size, font, and color. The news article tag lines are all different from the titles, but alike to each other. The gaps in the grid used allows the reader to see the repetition easily.

NPR.org
